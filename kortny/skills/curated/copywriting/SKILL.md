## Goal

Write copy that earns attention and drives the desired action — no filler, no jargon, no generic superlatives.

## Steps

1. **Clarify the brief** — what is this copy for? (Landing page, ad, email subject, product description, tagline.) Who is the audience? What's the one action they should take? What tone fits the brand?
2. **Ground in workspace facts** — pull known product positioning, brand voice, and ICP details from Kortny's knowledge graph if present. Do not invent brand claims.
3. **Draft multiple options** — for headlines and taglines, deliver at least 3 variants; for body copy, 1 polished draft. Label each with the angle it plays (emotional/rational/curiosity/urgency/social proof).
4. **Apply the copy checklist** from `references/copy-checklist.md` before delivering.
5. **Deliver in Slack** — short copy (≤100 words) goes inline; longer drafts go in a canvas or thread reply.
6. **Invite iteration** — close with: "Which angle resonates? I can push any of these further or try a different approach."

## Rules

- Every claim must be supportable. No fabricated metrics, awards, or customer counts.
- Avoid these words unless the brief requires them: "seamless", "robust", "powerful", "leverage", "revolutionize", "cutting-edge", "game-changing".
- One clear CTA per piece. Multiple CTAs = no CTA.
- Match the reading level to the audience. B2B enterprise buyers read at a different level than consumers.
- If the brand voice is defined in workspace facts, adhere to it. If undefined, ask before assuming.
