# FAQ Answers — Internal Comms Format

Vendored from anthropics/skills@57546260 under Apache-2.0, adapted for Slack delivery.

---

## Purpose

When employees are confused, asking the same questions in multiple channels, or responding to a big announcement with identical follow-up threads — an FAQ post surfaces the real questions and gives clear, authoritative answers. The goal: one read, no more repetition.

---

## What to cover

Focus on questions that affect a large portion of the team, not edge cases for one person. Typical high-signal areas:
- Recent corporate events (fundraises, new leadership hires, reorgs)
- Upcoming launches or significant product changes
- Changes to company policy, priorities, or direction
- Anything employees are evidently confused about based on their Slack messages

---

## Information gathering

Use connected tools where available:
- **Slack**: look for clusters of similar questions — threads with many replies or reactions to a post, the same question appearing in multiple channels, thumbs-up or +1 reactions on a question (signal that many others have the same one)
- **Documents**: official company announcements, policies, or strategy docs can serve as the authoritative source for answers
- **Calendar**: post-meeting questions in associated threads (especially from large-audience meetings)

If tools are unavailable, ask the user which questions they are seeing most often. They may supply the content directly.

---

## Scope

Be holistic — scan broadly, not just the user's own team or channel. Questions that matter to one team often matter to others. Aim for 5–10 questions that represent the broadest slice of the company.

---

## Format

```
*Question*: [One-sentence question as it would actually be asked]
*Answer*: [1–2 sentence answer, based on official sources where possible]
```

No preamble. Go straight into Q&A pairs. Post to Slack as a formatted message or offer a canvas version if there are more than ~8 questions or if they warrant a persistent reference.

---

## Answer quality guidelines

- Base answers on official company communications when possible; cite the source (link to doc, Slack post, or announcement)
- If the information is uncertain or not yet decided, say so plainly: "This hasn't been decided yet — [Owner] will share an update by [date]."
- Do not speculate or soften uncertainty with vague language
- Keep tone professional but direct — not a press release, not casual chat
- If a question requires executive input or an official decision, flag that: "This requires confirmation from [team/person]. An answer is being sought."
- One answer per question. No nested sub-questions.
