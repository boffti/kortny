## Goal

Write conversion-oriented "Product A vs Product B" comparison copy — the kind that captures bottom-of-funnel traffic and turns evaluators into customers.

## When to use

Use this when producing **marketing copy** (web pages, docs, PDFs) for a specific head-to-head comparison.
For **research-grade analysis**, use `competitive-analysis` or `competitor-profiling` instead.

## Steps

1. **Identify the matchup** — your product vs. which competitor. Confirm which audience (the competitor's customer who's evaluating a switch, or a net-new buyer).
2. **Establish the angle** — what does your product win on? Use workspace facts (known from Kortny's knowledge graph) about your product's positioning and differentiators. Do not invent claims.
3. **Research the competitor** — pull their pricing page, feature list, and common complaints (G2, Reddit, HN). Note the research date.
4. **Draft the comparison** using the structure in `references/comparison-page-structure.md`.
5. **Fact-check every claim** — especially competitor pricing and features. Flag anything you couldn't verify.
6. **Deliver** — post the draft as a Slack canvas for async review before it goes live. Add a "verify before publishing" note for any claim marked uncertain.

## Rules

- Never make false or misleading comparative claims. "X doesn't support Y" requires a source.
- Use "as of [date]" on competitor pricing — it changes.
- Acknowledge competitor strengths honestly in a "When [Competitor] might be the better fit" section — readers trust balanced comparisons more.
- Tone is confident, not snarky. Let the facts do the talking.
- Use workspace facts for your product's claims; do not fabricate specs.
