# Internal communications message types

## Announcement

**When to use**: new policy, org structure change, senior hire, product milestone.
**Tone**: direct, factual, forward-looking.
**Structure**: headline → what is changing → why → what it means for you → timeline → who to contact.
**Length**: 100-200 words for Slack post.

## Team update

**When to use**: weekly / sprint / project progress report.
**Tone**: matter-of-fact, no hype.
**Structure**: workstream grouping → what moved → what's blocked (name the blocker) → what's next.
**Length**: 200-300 words; bullets preferred.

## Sensitive message

**When to use**: workforce reduction, leadership transition, incident post-mortem shared broadly.
**Tone**: plain language, honest, no corporate euphemism.
**Structure**: acknowledge the impact first → facts → what happens next → what support is available.
**Length**: as short as complete honesty allows. Do not pad to seem thorough.

## Policy or process change

**When to use**: new expense policy, security requirement, tool migration, compliance change.
**Tone**: clear and helpful, not authoritative.
**Structure**: what is changing → effective date → what you need to do → where to find more information.
**Canvas pairing**: reference canvases (FAQs, runbooks) supplement the Slack post for anything employees will return to.

## Celebration / recognition

**When to use**: team milestone, product launch, external award.
**Tone**: genuine, brief. One sentence of why it matters, not just that it happened.
**Length**: under 80 words. No awards speech preamble.

---

## Scheduler pairing guide

Any recurring message type (weekly team update, monthly all-hands prep) can be paired with a Kortny schedule.
Suggested phrasing to offer users: "This message recurs — want me to set a schedule so it drafts automatically on [cadence]?"
