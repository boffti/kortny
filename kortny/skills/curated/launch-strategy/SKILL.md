## Goal

Produce a launch brief and coordinated rollout plan that gives every stakeholder the same picture of what's happening, when, and through which channels.

## Steps

1. **Define the launch** — what's being launched? Who is the primary audience? What's the launch window? What's the one thing you want people to remember or do?
2. **Establish success metrics** — what does a good launch look like? (Trial signups, coverage placements, pipeline generated, seats activated.) Lock these before writing copy.
3. **Sequence the launch channels** — use the `references/launch-channel-guide.md` to select and sequence channels. Not all channels for every launch.
4. **Draft the launch brief** — using the template in `references/launch-brief-template.md`. Populate it with what's known; flag what's missing.
5. **Post the brief** — share to the relevant Slack channel as a canvas so the team can comment asynchronously. Tag owners of each channel.
6. **Offer the next artifacts** — countdown schedule, press release draft, launch day checklist, or announcement copy — based on what the brief reveals is missing.

## Rules

- Lock the message before building the channel plan. Channel decisions made without a clear message create inconsistent launches.
- Every channel needs an owner and a date. "Someone will post on LinkedIn" is not a plan.
- Use workspace facts for product positioning, brand voice, and known customer names (logos, case studies) — do not invent them.
- Can pair with the scheduler: "post this announcement in #announcements on [date] at 9am." Mention this option when relevant.
- Do not over-engineer small feature launches. A one-pager and a Slack post may be the right plan.
