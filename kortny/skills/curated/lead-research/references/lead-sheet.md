# Lead sheet fields and qualification rubric

## Standard lead-sheet fields

Fill what you can verify; leave the rest blank-but-named ("headcount: unknown").
A named gap is information; a guessed value is a liability.

**Company**
- Name, what they do (one line), industry/segment.
- Size — headcount band and rough revenue if public.
- Stage / funding — last round, amount, date (flag if old).
- Location / market.
- Tech or vendor signals — tools they mention using (esp. competitors you
  displace or complements you integrate with).

**Contact**
- Name, title, and what they likely own/decide.
- Seniority and whether they're a decision-maker, influencer, or champion.
- How to reach (from CRM or public profile).
- CRM status — net-new, existing record, prior touches, current owner, open
  deals. Always check this when a CRM is connected.

**Fit signals** — why they match (or don't) the ideal customer profile: segment,
size, buyer type, use case, vendor stack.

**Intent / timing signals** — inbound activity, hiring that implies a need,
funding that implies budget, public statements of a problem you solve, renewal or
contract timing if known.

## Qualification rubric — Fit × Intent × Access

Read each lead on three axes, then give a single call.

- **Fit** — do they match the ICP? Segment, size, buyer, use case. High / partial
  / off-profile.
- **Intent** — is there a reason to act now? Inbound request, active search,
  budget/timing signal. Hot / warm / cold.
- **Access** — can the team actually reach the right person? Known decision-maker
  and a way in vs. a name with no path.

**The read:**
- **Strong fit** — high fit + warm/hot intent + a way in. Recommend a real next
  step (call, route to AE).
- **Worth a look** — good on one or two axes, gaps on the rest. Name what to
  confirm before investing.
- **Probably not** — off-profile, or no intent and no access. Say why, briefly,
  so the team can disagree with their own context.

Always state the **one or two facts** that drove the call. The rubric produces a
recommendation, not a verdict — the rep owns the decision.

## Using the CRM vs the web

- **CRM (when connected via Composio):** source of truth for prior contact, deal
  history, ownership, and existing firmographics. Check it first; it prevents
  duplicate outreach and tells you if the lead is already in play.
- **Web (always available):** for net-new leads, freshness (recent news,
  funding), and gaps the CRM lacks (current role, public signals). Use web search
  + the article-extractor skill for clean page text.
- **Attribute every fact** to its source (CRM record vs which URL) so freshness
  and reliability are visible.
