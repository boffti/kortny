# Cold Email Structure

## Initial email (target: 75-100 words)

**Subject:** [Trigger or result] — [Company name] (≤6 words)

**Line 1 — The hook:** One sentence tied to a specific, real observation about them. Reference the buying signal, their news, or their pain point. Not a generic compliment.

**Line 2 — The bridge:** One sentence connecting their situation to what you do. Don't explain the product — explain the outcome they'd get.

**Line 3 — The ask:** One low-friction question or CTA. "Worth 15 minutes?" or "Is this relevant?" — not "Can we schedule a demo at your earliest convenience?"

**Signature:** Name, title, company. No tagline, no logo, no wall of contact details.

---

## Follow-up 1 (send: 3-4 days later, target: 40-60 words)

Reference the previous email in one line. Add one new piece of value (a relevant resource, a short insight, a customer result). Keep the same ask or make it easier.

---

## Follow-up 2 — the break-up (send: 7-10 days later, target: 30-40 words)

"Last note from me on this — if the timing is off, totally understand. If it ever becomes relevant, [low-effort next step]."

---

## What makes the hook specific enough?
Good: "Saw you're hiring three data engineers — companies at that stage usually hit [pain point]."
Bad: "I see you're growing fast — congrats on the momentum!"

Good: "Your post about [topic] resonated — we had the same problem before we built [solution]."
Bad: "I've been following your content and I think there's a great fit here."
