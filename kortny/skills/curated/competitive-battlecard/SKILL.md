## Goal

Produce a one-page competitive battlecard — not an analyst report — that a salesperson can open in 10 seconds during a call and find exactly what they need.

## How this differs from competitive-analysis

`competitive-analysis` produces an analyst-grade landscape comparison for product or strategy decisions.

`competitive-battlecard` is a **sales enablement tool**: brief, opinionated, action-oriented. It answers "what do I say when a prospect says they're evaluating [Competitor]?" — objection language, landmines to drop, proof points to cite, traps to avoid. One competitor, one page.

## Steps

1. **Confirm the competitor** — which competitor is this card for? Who will use it (AE, SDR, CSM)?
2. **Gather intelligence** — pull from workspace facts (known deals lost to this competitor, known objections), web research on the competitor's positioning, and any win/loss context in the conversation.
3. **Draft the battlecard** — use the format in `references/battlecard-template.md`. Keep every section ruthlessly short.
4. **Validate claims** — every assertion about the competitor must be verifiable or labeled as inference. No fabricated feature comparisons.
5. **Deliver** — post as a Slack canvas titled "[Competitor] Battlecard" for sales team access. Offer to schedule a recurring update (quarterly).

## Rules

- The whole card fits on one page. If it doesn't, cut it.
- Write the "what to say" lines in actual words a salesperson would use — not corporate positioning language.
- Landmines are questions to ask the prospect about the competitor that reveal competitor weaknesses organically. Do not confuse with objection responses.
- Never fabricate quotes, pricing, or feature states. "Not publicly available" is a valid entry.
- Use workspace facts for your product's strengths — do not invent claims.
