# Key financial metrics by statement type

## Income statement

| Metric | Formula | Typical benchmark |
|---|---|---|
| Gross margin | (Revenue − COGS) / Revenue | Varies widely: SaaS 70-80%, manufacturing 20-40% |
| Operating margin | Operating income / Revenue | Healthy public company: 10-20%+ |
| Net margin | Net income / Revenue | Varies; negative is not always alarming for growth co |
| EBITDA margin | EBITDA / Revenue | Proxy for cash generation before structure |
| R&D as % of revenue | R&D expense / Revenue | SaaS: 15-25% typical |

## Balance sheet

| Metric | Formula | Signal |
|---|---|---|
| Current ratio | Current assets / Current liabilities | < 1.0 = liquidity risk |
| Quick ratio | (Cash + Short-term investments + AR) / Current liabilities | More conservative than current ratio |
| Debt-to-equity | Total debt / Shareholders' equity | > 2.0 warrants scrutiny |
| Cash as % of assets | Cash / Total assets | Higher = more flexibility |
| Book value per share | (Total equity − Preferred equity) / Shares outstanding | |

## Cash flow statement

| Metric | Formula | Signal |
|---|---|---|
| Free cash flow | Operating cash flow − Capex | Positive FCF = self-funding; negative = must raise or borrow |
| FCF margin | Free cash flow / Revenue | SaaS Rule of 40 pairing metric |
| Capex intensity | Capex / Revenue | High for asset-heavy businesses |
| Cash conversion | Operating CF / Net income | > 1.0 means earnings are well-backed by cash |

## Flags to always note

- **One-time items**: restructuring charges, gains on asset sales — exclude from run-rate analysis, but name them.
- **Stock-based compensation**: often added back to get to "adjusted" figures. Note the add-back amount.
- **Operating lease treatment**: IFRS 16 / ASC 842 moves leases onto the balance sheet — note if comparing pre- and post-adoption periods.
- **Restatements**: any prior-period restatement is material and must be flagged at the top of the analysis.
