# Copy Quality Checklist

Run through this before delivering any copy.

## Clarity
- [ ] The main idea is clear in the first sentence or headline.
- [ ] No unexplained acronyms or jargon for the target audience.
- [ ] A 12-year-old could understand what's being offered (even for technical copy — the *value* should be clear).

## Specificity
- [ ] At least one concrete detail (number, name, timeframe) that a generic piece wouldn't have.
- [ ] Claims are specific, not generic ("cuts reporting time by 40%" not "saves time").
- [ ] Customer proof (if used) is real and specific.

## Persuasion
- [ ] The reader's primary objection is acknowledged or addressed.
- [ ] Benefit language, not feature language (what it does for them, not what it is).
- [ ] One clear CTA — what should the reader do next?

## Brand fit
- [ ] Tone matches stated brand voice (or ask if undefined).
- [ ] No banned words from the style guide (check workspace facts).
- [ ] Product name spelled/formatted correctly per workspace facts.

## Mechanics
- [ ] No passive voice in headlines.
- [ ] No sentence starts with "We" in customer-facing copy (unless required).
- [ ] Headline under 10 words where possible; subheadline under 20.
