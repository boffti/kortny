# General Internal Communications

## Purpose

Use this guide for internal messages that do not fit the 3P, newsletter, or FAQ formats — for example: policy change notices, incident communications, onboarding announcements, team milestone posts, or ad-hoc leadership messages.

---

## Before writing: four questions to answer

1. **Who is the audience?** Specific team, a department, the whole company, a subset of a channel?
2. **What is the communication's purpose?** Inform, instruct, request action, acknowledge an event?
3. **What is the tone?** Formal, casual, urgent, reassuring?
4. **Are there specific formatting requirements?** Leadership template, brand voice, required disclaimers?

If any of these are unclear, ask one targeted question before drafting — don't guess and produce a message that needs to be redone.

---

## General principles

- **Lead with the most important information.** The first sentence should tell the reader what is happening or changing. Do not save the headline for the end.
- **Use active voice.** "We are changing the policy on X" not "It has been decided that the policy on X will be changed."
- **Be specific about actions and timelines.** "Reply to this thread by Friday" is better than "let us know your thoughts."
- **Close with a clear next step or contact.** Who do they reach out to? What do they do next? Make it explicit.

---

## Tone calibration by message type

| Type | Tone | Key elements |
|---|---|---|
| Policy change | Formal, factual | What changes, when it takes effect, what employees must do |
| Incident communication | Plain, direct | What happened, impact, current status, next update time |
| Milestone / celebration | Warm, energetic | What was achieved, who was involved, why it matters |
| Operational update | Neutral | What is changing, timeline, who to contact with questions |
| Sensitive (restructure, departure) | Respectful, plain | Acknowledge impact first; no spin; name the thing directly |

## Slack delivery

For short messages (under ~200 words): post directly as a Slack message with mrkdwn formatting.

For longer messages that employees will need to return to (policy docs, detailed FAQs, multi-step instructions): offer a canvas version.

If the message recurs on a schedule (weekly team update, monthly digest), note: "this can be paired with a recurring schedule — ask me to set that up."

---

## Checklist before sending

- [ ] First sentence answers "what is happening"
- [ ] Audience is correct (not too broad, not too narrow)
- [ ] Tone matches the message type
- [ ] No jargon or internal acronyms unexplained
- [ ] Clear next step or contact at the end
- [ ] No filler openings ("I'm excited to share…", "As we continue our journey…")
