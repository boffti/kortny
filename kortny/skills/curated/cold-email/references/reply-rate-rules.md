# Reply-Rate Rules

Checklist before sending any cold email.

## The 5 killers (instant delete)
- [ ] Opens with "I hope this email finds you well" or any throat-clearing.
- [ ] Subject line is vague ("Quick question", "Introduction", "Checking in").
- [ ] First sentence is about you or your company, not about them.
- [ ] More than one ask.
- [ ] Over 150 words.

## The 5 boosters
- [ ] Subject line has a specific name, number, or event hook.
- [ ] First sentence references something provably specific about the recipient.
- [ ] The ask is a yes/no or single click — not a scheduling commitment.
- [ ] Social proof appears without bragging: "We helped [similar company] do X."
- [ ] Sends Tuesday–Thursday, 7am–10am in recipient's timezone if known.

## A/B variables worth testing (one at a time)
- Subject line: question vs. statement vs. result
- Hook: news-based vs. signal-based vs. problem-based
- CTA: calendar link vs. "worth a chat?" vs. "is this relevant?"
- Length: 60 words vs. 100 words
- Sender name: first name only vs. first + last

## When to stop the sequence
- After 3 touches with no response: move to nurture or discard.
- Any explicit "not interested": honor immediately, no breakup email.
- Unsubscribe: remove permanently, no exceptions.
