## Goal

Write cold emails that earn replies — not emails that get deleted. Short, human, one ask, anchored to a specific reason for reaching out.

## Steps

1. **Identify the target** — who is this to? What's their role, company, and the specific reason for reaching out now? (A buying signal, a news item, a shared connection, a job posting — the "why now".)
2. **Check workspace facts** — use known product positioning, ICP, and any existing account context from Kortny's knowledge graph.
3. **Draft the email(s)** — see structure in `references/cold-email-structure.md`. If a sequence is requested, draft the initial + 2 follow-ups.
4. **Apply reply-rate rules** from `references/reply-rate-rules.md`.
5. **Deliver** — post inline to Slack (emails are short); offer to adjust tone or angle if needed.

## Rules

- No email over 100 words for the initial outreach. Follow-ups can be even shorter.
- One clear ask per email. Multiple asks = zero replies.
- Personalization must be real: "I saw your job posting for a data engineer" is real. "I noticed you're a leader in your space" is not.
- Never fabricate mutual connections or claim false familiarity.
- Subject line under 6 words.
- No "I hope this email finds you well."
